🍝 Pink Sauce Protein Pasta

Serves 2

Ingredients

Pasta + broccoli
  • Brami high-protein pasta 6–8 oz dry
  • Broccoli florets 2 cups
  • Salt (for pasta water) 1–2 tsp
Pink protein sauce
  • Rao's marinara 1 cup
  • Cottage cheese ¾ cup
  • Milk ¼ cup +
  • Italian seasoning ½ tsp
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Red pepper flakes optional
  • Parmesan 2 tbsp, opt.

Method

  1. Bring a medium pot of salted water to a boil. Add Brami pasta, cook per box directions.
  2. In the last 3 minutes, drop 2 cups broccoli into the same pot.
  3. Before draining, save ½ cup pasta water. Drain pasta + broccoli.
  4. Blend: 1 cup Rao's, ¾ cup cottage cheese, ¼ cup milk, ½ tsp Italian seasoning, black pepper.
  5. Pour sauce into the empty pot, heat low / medium-low 3–5 min. Don't hard-boil it.
  6. Add pasta + broccoli back, stir. Splash in pasta water if too thick.
  7. Top with parmesan + red pepper flakes.

🔥 Gochujang variant (the one you loved)

Add 2 tbsp gochujang into the blended sauce for a spicy-savory Korean spin. Skip the olive oil — you don't need it.

⭐ Make it gourmet (next-level)

  1. Bloom & toast: melt ½ tbsp butter + a little oil, sauté 2–3 cloves minced garlic 30 sec, then fry the gochujang + a spoon of marinara in that fat 60–90 sec until it darkens — this is the Korean-rosé depth. Then blend as usual.
  2. Balance: a pinch of sugar or ½ tsp honey to round the heat.
  3. Char the broccoli in a hot dry/oiled pan till spotty-brown instead of boiling it.
  4. Emulsify off-heat: kill the flame, add parm + a splash of pasta water, toss hard so it goes glossy.
  5. Finish: torn fresh basil, black pepper, a few drops of lemon to brighten.

Serve with garlic bread — toast sourdough in the pizza maker, rub with cut garlic, brush butter, dust parm + Italian seasoning. (Or just heat the garlic naan dippers.)

Protein boost: the cottage cheese already does the work — small curd is fine here since it's blended smooth. Add ½ cup Fage 0% on the side for ~20g more.

🌶️ Chilli Paneer

1–2 people · with bell peppers & broccoli

Ingredients

  • Paneer, cubed 200–250 g
  • Bell pepper, squares ½–1
  • Broccoli, small florets 1–1½ cups
  • Cornstarch (coating) 2 tbsp
  • Cornstarch slurry 1 tsp + 2 tbsp water
  • Soy sauce 1 tbsp
  • Chili sauce 1½–2 tbsp
  • Ketchup 2 tbsp
  • Black pepper ½ tsp total
  • Oil 2–3 tbsp
  • Water ¼ cup +
Optional if you have: onion, garlic, ginger, green chili, vinegar, sugar, salt.

Method

  1. Prep broccoli: microwave/boil 1–2 min just to soften. Keep it bright green & firm — not mushy.
  2. Coat paneer: toss cubes with 2 tbsp cornstarch, ¼ tsp black pepper, tiny splash of water — thin coating.
  3. Pan-fry paneer: 2–3 tbsp oil, medium heat, 4–6 min until lightly golden on a few sides. Remove. Don't over-fry or it goes chewy (and over-fried paneer drinks more sauce later).
  4. Stir-fry veg: same pan, bell peppers + broccoli, medium-high 2–3 min. Want them slightly charred, not watery. Pinch of black pepper.
  5. Sauce: lower to medium, add 1 tbsp soy, 1½–2 tbsp chili sauce, 2 tbsp ketchup, ¼ cup water, ¼ tsp black pepper. Bubble 30–60 sec.
  6. Thicken: stir in slurry (1 tsp cornstarch + 2 tbsp water). Sauce turns glossy & slightly thick.
  7. Finish: return paneer, toss 1–2 min until everything is coated.

Sauce ratio to memorize: 2 tbsp ketchup + 2 tbsp chili sauce + 1 tbsp soy + ¼ cup water.
More ketchup = sweeter · more chili = spicier · more soy = saltier · more water = saucier · more slurry = thicker.

🍚 Broccoli-Paneer Rice Bowl

Make rice. Sauté broccoli & paneer separately. Plate rice as a bed and spoon the saucy paneer on top — don't fold it all together.

Keep it drenched (rice eats the sauce): rice wicks up sauce and dries the paneer, especially if it sits. Fixes — ① make 1.5–2× the sauce so there's enough for both · ② serve rice on the side / as a bed, not mixed in · ③ hold back ⅓ of the sauce and toss the paneer in it right before serving · ④ thicken slightly (more slurry) so it clings instead of running into the rice.
Cut the calories: swap most of the frying oil for 0-cal cooking spray — Sheetal says do a mix of spray + a little oil so it still browns.

⭐ Crispy paneer without the tedium

You don't need all 6 sides crispy — the sauce coats everything, so 2–3 golden faces is plenty. Pick a method:

  1. Air fryer (hands-off, best for a big batch): cornstarch-coated cubes + a little oil/spray, single layer, 400°F ~10–12 min, shake once. Or oven/broiler at 425°F, flip once. Lowest calorie too.
  2. If you stay at the pan: pat the paneer dry first · rip-hot pan, single layer, don't crowd · leave it alone 2–3 min (it releases on its own once crusted — if it's fighting you, it's not ready) · flip in rows with a wide spatula or just shake/toss the pan to roll them all at once.
  3. No lid: covering traps steam — soft, pale, gummy coating. Paneer is already cooked; you're only browning the surface, so a lid speeds up nothing.

(Slab-then-cube doesn't work here — the coating goes on before frying, so cut faces would be bare.)

🍕 Pizza-Maker Pizza

In Aashay's red pizza maker · 400°F

Ingredients

  • Thin-crust pizza base 1
  • Rao's sauce thin layer
  • Cottage cheese (drained) dollops
  • Mozzarella to top
  • Kalamata olives
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Bell peppers
  • Feta a little
  • Fresh basil at the end
Season the cottage cheese
  • Salt · black pepper
  • Italian seasoning · chili flakes

Method

  1. Drain cottage cheese in a paper towel to pull out extra water, then mix in salt, black pepper, Italian seasoning & chili flakes.
  2. Spread a thin layer of Rao's sauce on the base.
  3. Dollop the seasoned cottage cheese around like ricotta — not covering the whole pizza, just spots.
  4. Add toppings: olives, cherry tomatoes, bell peppers. Add feta.
  5. Top with mozzarella.
  6. Bake ~400°F, 5-min timer, then check. Pizza maker gets hot fast — peek every 30 sec so the bottom doesn't burn.
  7. Chop fresh basil and add at the very end — otherwise it turns black.

🍕 Paneer Tikka Pizza (fusion)

Swap the cottage cheese for tikka-marinated paneer cubes + red onion + bell peppers over a thin layer of tikka sauce, top with mozzarella, bake, then finish with a yogurt drizzle (no cilantro — Aashay). Two things you already make, combined.

Shortcut: a pre-made margherita-style base means you just add toppings & bake.

🍛 Paneer Tikka Masala

1–2 people · with naan dippers or rice
This is exactly what Auntie meant by "blend a curry sauce + yogurt for the gravy." Rich and restaurant-style — and it fixes the wrap problem (no wrapping, the gravy is the point).

Ingredients

  • Paneer, cubed 200–250 g
  • TJ tikka masala sauce ~1 cup
  • Fage 0% yogurt 2–3 tbsp
  • Bell pepper + onion optional
  • Butter 1 tsp
  • Garlic, minced 1–2 cloves
  • Water splash
  • Kasoori methi / garam masala if you have
Protein: the yogurt thickens and adds protein — Auntie's two-birds move. Want more? Stir in an extra ¼ cup Fage at the end.

Method

  1. Marinate paneer cubes in 2–3 tbsp of the tikka sauce, 15–30 min.
  2. Melt butter, sauté garlic + onion/peppers until soft.
  3. Add the rest of the tikka sauce + a splash of water, simmer 3–4 min.
  4. Lower the heat (important), then stir in the yogurt to make it creamy. Don't let it boil after — temper the yogurt with a spoon of hot sauce first so it won't split.
  5. Add the marinated paneer, simmer gently 3–4 min. Crush kasoori methi over the top if you have it.
  6. Finish with a swirl of yogurt + a touch of butter — no cilantro (Aashay hates it). Serve with warm naan dippers or rice.

🍚 Paneer Tikka Bowl (drier, plate-able)

Skip the gravy step — just char the marinated paneer + peppers in the pan, serve over rice with a drizzle of yogurt-tikka sauce, cucumber & lettuce on the side. Higher-protein, lower-cal, looks gorgeous.

🫘 Chana Masala

2–3 people · highest protein-per-dollar

Ingredients

  • Canned chickpeas 2 cans, drained
  • Onion, chopped 1
  • Tomato or curry/tikka sauce 1 cup
  • Ginger + garlic 1 tbsp each
  • Oil / butter 1 tbsp
  • Chana masala or garam masala 1–2 tsp
  • Cumin ½ tsp
  • Salt · lemon to finish
Protein: ~15 g per cup of chickpeas, no animal product needed. Stir in Fage at the end for a creamier, even higher-protein version.

Method

  1. Sauté cumin in oil 20 sec, add onion + ginger + garlic, cook till golden.
  2. Add tomato/curry sauce + spices, cook 3–4 min until it darkens and the oil separates.
  3. Add chickpeas + a splash of water, simmer 8–10 min. Lightly mash a few chickpeas to thicken.
  4. Finish with lemon — skip the cilantro (Aashay). Serve with rice or naan dippers.

🍲 Same idea, other beans

Rajma (canned kidney beans) or dal (red lentils, ~20 min on the stove) work the same way — tons of protein. Great rotation when you want a break from paneer.

🌯 Paneer Wraps

Two versions
Wrap tearing / too plain? Warm the tortilla 15 sec under a damp paper towel so it doesn't crack, crumble or cut the paneer small, keep filling in a line off-center, and add a creamy sauce to bind. Easiest fix: fold it in half as a quesadilla and crisp it in the pizza maker instead of rolling a burrito. For flavor, the tikka masala or chili paneer beat a plain wrap every time.

Version 1 — Gochujang Creamy Wrap

Creamy sauce
  • Fage 0% yogurt base
  • Gochujang
  • Salt
  • Ketchup 1 tsp
Paneer filling
  • Mixed-color bell peppers sautéed
  • Paneer
  • Soy sauce 1 tbsp
  • Chili sauce 1 tsp
  • Ketchup 1 tsp
  • Tortilla + lettuce
  1. Mix the creamy sauce: Fage + gochujang + salt + a tsp ketchup.
  2. Sauté mixed bell peppers, add paneer + soy + chili sauce + ketchup. Don't over-cook the paneer — it doesn't need to brown.
  3. Warm the tortilla, spread the creamy sauce, add the paneer mix & lettuce. Roll.

Version 2 — Paneer Tikka Wrap

  • TJ's tikka masala sauce
  • Paneer cubes
  • Bell peppers
  • Greek yogurt (Fage) for creaminess
  • Tortilla
  1. Coat paneer cubes well in tikka sauce, let marinate 30 min.
  2. Sauté the peppers in that same sauce, then add the marinated paneer. No cornstarch, no pre-frying needed.
  3. While sautéing, stir in some yogurt to make the tikka creamy. Add a little more gravy if you want it saucier.
  4. It's already saucy — no extra spread needed. Fill the tortilla & roll.
Note from Sheetal: she suggested doing paneer wraps over bean burritos — but the bean version is saved under Tips if you want it.

🧈 Crispy Tofu Side

Tastes almost like chilli paneer

Ingredients

  • Nasoya extra-firm tofu cubed
  • Oil ½ tbsp
  • Soy sauce
  • Chili sauce
  • Ketchup very little
  • Sweet Thai chili sauce ~1 tsp
Why Nasoya: the packet kind is so firm you don't even need to press out water. Cut into normal-size cubes.

Method

  1. Heat ½ tbsp oil, add tofu cubes.
  2. Once slightly brown on all sides, add soy sauce, chili sauce, very little ketchup, and ~1 tsp sweet Thai chili sauce.
  3. Toss to coat — serve on the side.

A fresh spin on the same tofu, made into a full meal over rice.

Glaze

2 tbsp gochujang · 1 tbsp soy · 1 tsp sweet chili garlic sauce · 1 tsp honey · 1 tsp rice vinegar (or lemon) · splash of water

🌮 Black Bean & Cheese Folded Taquitos

Makes 6–8 · new from Sheetal (Jul 13)

Ingredients

  • Small corn tortillas (or flour for softer) 6–8
  • Black beans, canned, drained & rinsed 1 can (15 oz)
  • Shredded Mexican blend / Monterey Jack / cheddar 1–1½ cups
  • TJ's taco seasoning (or 1 tsp smoked paprika) 1 tsp
  • Oregano ½ tsp
  • Salt · black pepper to taste
  • Salsa 2–3 tbsp, optional
  • Neutral oil or butter 1–2 tbsp
Chipotle-yogurt dip
  • Greek yogurt ½ cup
  • Chipotle sauce (bottled) 2 tbsp
  • Salt pinch
Canned beans while the Instant Pot's down. Sheetal's fresh-bean method for later: wash well, beans + double the water + salt, 30 min in the IP, don't open early.

Method

  1. Filling: mash about half the beans with a fork, leave the rest whole for texture. Mix in the taco seasoning, oregano, salt + pepper (and salsa for moisture if using). Fold in the cheese.
  2. Warm each tortilla 15–20 sec so it's flexible and doesn't crack.
  3. ~2 tbsp filling on one half, fold into a half-moon (not rolled), press gently.
  4. Brush the outsides with a little oil, then skillet over medium: 3–4 min per side, pressing gently with the spatula, until golden and melty.
  5. Extra-crispy trick: stand them up on the folded edge 30–60 sec so that edge crisps and the cheese fully melts.
  6. Dip: stir the yogurt + chipotle sauce + pinch of salt (alt version: Taco Bell hot sauce + Italian seasoning + salt). Lettuce on the side.

🧆 Falafel Wraps

3 falafel per wrap · new from Sheetal (Jul 13)

Ingredients

  • TJ's frozen falafel balls 3 per wrap
  • Flour tortillas (freezer ones fine) 1 per wrap
  • TJ's hummus — chili onion flavor to spread
  • Small cucumber, peeled 1 — small only
  • Lettuce chopped small
  • Cherry tomatoes chopped small
  • Kalamata olives chopped small
Creamy white sauce
  • Greek yogurt base
  • Salt · Italian seasoning
  • Any chili sauce 1 tsp
Buy the small cucumber — the leftover from a big one goes bad fast. The chili onion hummus is the family favorite.

Method

  1. Warm the falafel in the pizza maker on LOW — warm all the way through without burning the outside.
  2. Chopped salad: lettuce, tomatoes, peeled cucumber, kalamata — everything chopped small.
  3. White sauce: Greek yogurt + salt + Italian seasoning + a teaspoon of chili sauce.
  4. Warm the tortillas on the skillet right before eating — do 4–5 at a time.
  5. Build: spread hummus on the tortilla, drizzle white sauce, lightly press the falafel balls with your hand and lay in 3, add the chopped salad, drizzle more white sauce (+ chili sauce if you want spicy).
  6. Roll it tight.

🍜 Chili-Garlic Noodles with Crispy Tofu

Full batch = dinner for 2 · ~900–950 cal/bowl · Rishi original
⭐ Rishi's rule: more sauce. The amounts below are the floor — scale the whole sauce bowl 1.5–2×, and extra chili garlic sauce makes it better.

Ingredients

Crispy tofu
  • TJ's Super Firm High-Protein Tofu 1 block (16 oz)
  • Soy sauce 1 tbsp
  • Cornstarch 2 tbsp
  • White pepper ⅓ tsp
Noodles + veg
  • TJ's Squiggly Knife Cut Noodles 2 cakes
  • Yellow onion, chopped ½
  • Broccoli florets 1½ cups
  • Red bell pepper, sliced 1
  • TJ's shredded carrots ½ cup
  • Neutral oil 2 tbsp, divided
  • Cooking spray as needed
  • Green onions, sliced 2, to top
Sauce (scale it up ⬆)
  • Soy sauce 1 tbsp
  • TJ's frozen minced garlic 4 cubes (or 4 cloves)
  • Chili garlic sauce 1½ tbsp + more
  • TJ's Chili Onion Crunch 1 tbsp
  • Black pepper, fresh ⅓ tsp
  • Brown sugar 2 tsp
  • Rice vinegar 2 tsp
  • Toasted sesame oil 2 tsp

Method

  1. Prep the tofu: pat very dry, cut into 1" cubes — no pressing needed. Toss with 1 tbsp soy. Stir the white pepper into the cornstarch, sprinkle over, toss until evenly coated.
  2. Crisp it: 1 tbsp oil in a large nonstick over medium-high. Single layer, mist the tops with spray. Undisturbed 3–4 min, then flip (spraying as you turn) until golden all over, 10–12 min total. Set aside.
  3. Fork-mix the sauce — no heat, no blender: soy, garlic, chili garlic sauce, Chili Onion Crunch, black pepper, brown sugar, rice vinegar, sesame oil.
  4. Noodles: boil per package (~4 min), drain, rinse briefly so they don't stick.
  5. Stir-fry: remaining 1 tbsp oil, same skillet, high heat. Onion 1–2 min, then broccoli + bell pepper + carrots until crisp-tender, ~4 min.
  6. Toss: pour in the sauce and let it sizzle 30 sec so the garlic blooms. Add noodles, toss 1–2 min until glossy. Taste — more pepper or a splash of soy if needed. Top with the crispy tofu + green onions, split into two bowls.
No added salt on purpose — soy + chili garlic + Chili Onion Crunch carry it; adjust at the very end. White pepper is the classic stir-fried-noodle heat — sharper and funkier than black. Two noodle cakes ≈ 700 cal total.

🫓 Paneer Tikka Flatbread Pizzas

Makes 2 · ~1,100 cal & ~40g protein each · Rishi original

Ingredients

Paneer + marinade
  • Paneer, ¾" cubes 8 oz
  • Fage 0% yogurt ⅓ cup
  • Tikka masala sauce 2 tbsp (from below)
  • Minced garlic 1½ tsp
  • Red pepper flakes ½ tsp
Creamy tikka sauce
  • Tikka masala sauce ¾–1 cup total, divided
  • Low-fat cottage cheese ⅓ cup
Build
  • Flatbread crusts 2
  • Olive oil, to brush 1 tsp
  • Lite mozzarella, shredded ⅔ cup
  • Red bell pepper, thin-sliced 1
  • Yellow onion, diced ½ cup
  • Grape tomatoes, halved ¾ cup
  • Fresh basil, torn 12 leaves

Method

  1. Marinate: pat the paneer dry. Mix yogurt + 2 tbsp tikka sauce + garlic + pepper flakes, toss the cubes, rest 15–20 min while the oven heats to 425°F.
  2. Creamy sauce: blend the remaining tikka sauce with the cottage cheese until smooth — blender, or fork-mash + whisk. (Small curd is fine — it's blended.)
  3. Pre-toast the crusts — the step that saves the texture: brush lightly with oil (½ tsp each), bake bare 3–4 min at 425°F until just starting to crisp. No soggy middle.
  4. Broil the paneer: switch to high broil, foil-lined tray, 5–7 min until charred at the edges, flipping halfway. Watch it — golden → burnt happens fast.
  5. Sauce both crusts (oven back to 425°F), spread evenly, leave a small border.
  6. Assemble — split evenly across both: mozzarella first, then paneer, bell pepper, onion, tomatoes.
  7. Bake 7–9 min until the cheese melts and the edges are deep golden — pre-toasted crusts finish a minute or two fast.
  8. Finish: torn basil over both, extra pepper flakes if you want heat, slice.
Zero-calorie flavor move: rub the hot toasted crust with a cut garlic clove before saucing.
Per pizza: ~1,100 cal · ~40g protein (crust 560 · paneer ~360 · mozz ~90 · sauce/marinade ~65 · oil ~20 · veg ~30).

🍔 Paneer Tikka Burgers

Makes 2 · same system as the tikka flatbreads
Same method as the flatbread pizzas, burger-shaped: identical yogurt-tikka marinade, identical broil-till-charred — just on two thick slabs instead of cubes. A patty has no cornstarch coating, so the slab sears/broils clean: two flips, no forming, nothing crumbles.

Ingredients

Patties + marinade (the flatbread marinade)
  • Paneer, cut into 2 thick slabs (¾–1") 1 block (~200 g)
  • Fage 0% yogurt 3 tbsp
  • Tikka masala sauce 1½ tbsp
  • Minced garlic 1 tsp
  • Red pepper flakes ¼ tsp
  • Pepper jack slices 2
Creamy tikka spread (the flatbread sauce)
  • Greek yogurt (or blended cottage cheese) 3 tbsp
  • Tikka masala sauce 2 tsp
  • Salt pinch
Build
  • Burger buns (TJ brioche/potato) 2
  • Lettuce
  • Tomato, sliced & seeded
  • Red onion, thin optional, pickled

Method

  1. Marinate the slabs exactly like the flatbread paneer: pat dry, poke a few times with a fork, coat in yogurt + tikka + garlic + flakes, 15–30 min.
  2. Quick-pickle the onion (optional): thin red onion + splash of vinegar or lime + pinch of salt, sits while you cook.
  3. Broil like the flatbread: high broil on a foil-lined tray, 5–7 min, flip halfway, until charred at the edges. Watch it — golden → burnt happens fast. (No broiler? Sear in a lightly oiled skillet 2–3 min/side.)
  4. Melt: pepper jack on each slab for the last 30–60 sec under the broiler.
  5. Toast the buns in the pizza maker till golden.
  6. Spread: yogurt + tikka + salt — the flatbread's creamy sauce as a burger spread. (Making flatbreads the same week? Reserve some of that sauce — it's this, ready-made.)
  7. Build: spread both bun faces → lettuce → seeded tomato → charred tikka slab with melted cheese → pickled onion → lid on.
Protein play: ~100 g paneer per burger; low-fat paneer from the Indian store makes this genuinely lean. Extra spread doubles as a side dip.

🔀 Other directions

Chipotle version: swap the spread for the taquito dip (yogurt + chipotle + salt). Smash-style: grate paneer, mash with 1 tbsp cornstarch + the marinade spices, form thin patties, sear — more burger-y texture, more work, crumblier. Slab first.

Plating & Presentation

Restaurant-level look with zero extra ingredients. Start with the 6 rules, then the per-dish moves.

🔥
Hot food, warm plate

Rinse the plate under hot water, dry it, and wipe the rim clean right before serving. A smudged rim ruins everything.

🗻
Height + empty edges

Build up in the center, leave the rim bare. Negative space reads as expensive — don't spread food to fill the plate.

⚫
Right plate

Pale or matte-black plates make color pop. Use wide shallow bowls for pasta, chili paneer & the tofu bowl.

3️⃣
Odd & off-center

Three paneer cubes face-up, set off-center — not a centered grid. Asymmetry looks intentional.

🌿
Garnish last

Torn basil, microplaned parm "snow," chili flakes, sesame, a thin drizzle — added the second before it hits the table.

✨
Texture on top

Keep something charred or crunchy sitting above the sauce, visible — not buried in it.

DishPlate it like this
🍝 PastaTwirl into a tall nest using tongs + a ladle, center of a wide bowl. Spoon extra sauce around the base, lean charred broccoli on the nest, finish with parm snow, torn basil, chili flakes & a drip of oil.
🌶️ Chili paneerShallow bowl, glossy sauce clinging, piled with height. Sesame + sliced green chili/scallion on top. Serve rice separately as a clean dome (pack into a cup, invert).
🍚 Rice bowlDome the rice to one side, arrange paneer & broccoli in their own zones (no mush), drizzle sauce only over the paneer.
🍕 PizzaFresh basil after baking, a drizzle + flaky salt, cut clean, serve on a board or dark plate.
🥪 SandwichCut on a sharp diagonal, stack the halves slightly offset so the layered cross-section faces out. That's the whole shot.
🧈 Tofu bowlGlossy mounded tofu + rice dome + broccoli, then sesame, scallion & a lime wedge on the rim.
If you photograph it: shoot in natural light near a window, either straight top-down or at a low 45° angle. Never overhead kitchen light — it goes yellow and flat.

Techniques & Tips

🚫
No cilantro, ever

Aashay hates it — Sheetal's rule: don't put it in anything. Garnish with fresh basil or green onions instead.

💪
Instant +20g protein

Half a cup of Fage 0% added to any meal gives ~20g protein with zero work.

🥛
Chaas with every meal

A cup of chaas alongside a meal adds protein effortlessly — and it's good for you.

🫗
Spray + oil mix

To cut calories when frying, mix 0-cal cooking spray with a little real oil so food still browns.

🧊
Freeze it

Going home for the weekend? Freeze leftover paneer, cottage cheese, bread & tortillas so nothing spoils. Vegetarian cheeses keep a long time frozen.

🥦
Three core veg

Stock romaine, broccoli & bell peppers — they work in pasta, any Indian gravy, pizza, and Asian food.

🍛
Paneer butter masala

Blend a curry sauce with yogurt and you've got the gravy for paneer butter masala.

🫘
Bean & cheese burrito (backup)

Canned black beans warmed with salt, tortillas, cheddar, Taco Bell hot sauce, romaine on the side — or make the folded taquitos instead.

🍅
Seed the tomatoes

Remove tomato seeds before adding to sandwiches/wraps so they don't go watery.
